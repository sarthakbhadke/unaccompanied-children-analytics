## UAC System Capacity & Care Load Analytics

**For:** Government & Policy Stakeholders

## Purpose
This report summarizes findings from a data analytics study of the
Unaccompanied Alien Children (UAC) care pipeline (2023–2025),
covering 720 days of operational data from the U.S. Department of
Health & Human Services.

---

## What We Found

### The system reached a historic crisis in December 2023
At its peak on December 20, 2023, **11,516 children** were
simultaneously in HHS care — the highest recorded in the dataset.
One in four reporting days across 3 years qualified as a
"high-stress" day (care load above 8,010 children).

### The system recovered dramatically in early 2025
By August 2025, care load had fallen to an average of 2,001
children — a **78.4% reduction** from the December 2023 peak.
This contraction was driven primarily by reduced border intake
rather than increased discharge processing.

### Discharges consistently outpaced intake
Across the full timeline, 124,853 children were discharged to
sponsors against 92,641 transfers into HHS care — a
**Discharge Offset Ratio of 1.35**. For every child entering
HHS care, 1.35 children were successfully placed with sponsors.

### 2024 was the pressure year
2024 saw the highest single-day apprehensions (333 on Feb 15, 2024)
while HHS simultaneously worked to discharge the inherited 2023
population. Net intake in 2024 was nearly balanced (+863 net),
making it the most operationally strained year.

## Recommendations

**1. Establish a real-time monitoring threshold**
Activate emergency protocols when HHS care load exceeds 8,000
children. This threshold was breached for 180 days across the
study period.

**2. Pre-position resources for August–December**
Historical data shows consistent care load spikes in this window
across both 2023 and 2024. Proactive staffing and shelter
capacity planning can prevent reactive overcrowding.

**3. Accelerate sponsor matching during intake surges**
February 2024 demonstrated that high CBP intake coinciding with
elevated HHS load creates maximum system strain. Discharge
processing speed is the primary lever for relief.

**4. Deploy the analytics dashboard**
The Streamlit dashboard developed in this study enables continuous
monitoring of all key indicators with daily data updates. Deployment
to government stakeholders is recommended for ongoing situational
awareness.
